Introduction

Workshop Overview

This session explores how curated AI tools can streamline drafting, visual explanations, and awareness materials for government contexts—always with human oversight and without touching operational data.

  • Awareness-focused
  • Human-centred
  • Policy-aligned

Context

Why This Workshop

AI literacy helps scientific and government teams explain ideas clearly, produce consistent outreach, and prototype responsibly. The focus here is support and clarity—not automation, decision-making, or data processing.

Audience

Who Can Benefit

Scientists
Technical Officers
Administrative Staff
Project Teams

Approach

How AI Is Used in This Workshop

Drafting assistance
Visual explanation
Prototyping support
Awareness material creation
No data processing. No decision-making. Human review remains essential.

Tool Highlights

AI Tools Covered

Codex

Supports prototyping by suggesting code patterns and snippets to speed iteration.

Safe usage: use only in sandbox environments; keep sensitive repos and credentials out.

Napkin.ai

Creates diagram concepts that communicate flows and processes clearly.

Safe usage: avoid internal network details; keep diagrams conceptual and non-sensitive.

Image Generation AI

Produces poster-ready awareness concepts to support engagement.

Safe usage: label AI-assisted images; verify alignment with branding and policy.

Outcomes

Key Takeaways

  • Recognise where AI drafting and visuals can save time without replacing oversight.
  • Shape prompts that avoid sensitive information while improving clarity.
  • Apply human verification and record AI-assisted steps for transparency.
  • Follow governance pathways before adopting tools beyond demonstrations.

Guardrails

Responsible & Safe Use of AI

  • Do not enter sensitive, personal, or operational data.
  • Keep humans accountable for all outputs; verify accuracy and appropriateness.
  • Follow organisational security, privacy, and procurement policies.
  • Use pre-approved tools; log prompts and outputs where required.
  • Redact specifics; rely on generic or scenario-based examples.
  • Seek legal and security guidance before any integration or rollout.
