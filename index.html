Introduction

Workshop Overview

This session explores practical, policy-aware use of AI to boost productivity for government and scientific teams. It focuses on awareness, safe experimentation, and human-led evaluation rather than operational deployment.

  • Awareness-focused: guided examples and prompts only.
  • Human-centred: people remain accountable for choices and outputs.
  • Policy-aligned: respects security, privacy, and procurement controls.

Context

Why This Workshop

AI literacy helps scientific and government teams frame problems, evaluate AI-supported options, and communicate clearly. The aim is to understand possibilities while keeping oversight, transparency, and security at the core.

The workshop shows how AI can assist with drafting, explaining, and visualising ideas. It does not automate decisions or process institutional data; every output is validated by people.

Participants

Who Can Benefit

Scientists

Translate research ideas into clear summaries and visual explainers for varied audiences.

Technical Officers

Create policy-ready drafts, checklists, and scenario prompts while keeping systems secure.

Administrative Staff

Produce concise briefs, notices, and awareness material with consistent tone and clarity.

Project Teams

Align messages, diagrams, and prototypes so stakeholders share a common view of the work.

Approach

How AI Is Used in This Workshop

  • Drafting assistance for briefs, checklists, and summaries with human edits.
  • Visual explanation through simplified diagrams and storyboards for clarity.
  • Prototyping support to outline options quickly and gather feedback.
  • Awareness material creation for posters and slides with clear attribution.
  • No data processing and no automated decision-making; people approve everything.

Tool Highlights

AI Tools Covered

Codex

Speeds up drafting, prototyping, and exploration of code or content patterns.

Safe use: keep experiments isolated; review outputs line-by-line.

Napkin.ai

Generates clean diagrams to communicate workflows, safeguards, and service options.

Safe use: depict generic flows only; omit network or system specifics.

Image Generation AI

Creates illustrative visuals and posters to support awareness activities.

Safe use: label AI-assisted graphics; keep imagery non-sensitive and neutral.

Outcomes

Key Takeaways

  • Understand where AI can responsibly assist everyday tasks.
  • Practice clear prompting that respects confidentiality boundaries.
  • Apply governance-first thinking to AI-supported outputs.
  • Know when to seek approvals, security review, and expert oversight.
  • Keep human validation central before sharing or acting on results.

Guardrails

Responsible & Safe Use of AI

  • Government-focused guardrails: adhere to security classifications and data handling rules.
  • Data minimisation: redact details, keep prompts abstract, and avoid personal information.
  • Human verification: double-check accuracy, relevance, and tone before distribution.
  • Policy adherence: follow organisational approvals, procurement pathways, and record keeping.
  • Transparency: label AI-assisted content and cite sources where applicable.
