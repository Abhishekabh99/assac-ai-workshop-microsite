Introduction

Workshop Overview

This companion page supports the ASSAC training session on AI tools for productivity. It demonstrates how curated AI services can streamline drafting, visual explanations, and awareness materials while emphasizing safe, policy-aligned usage.

  • Awareness-focused: guidance and examples only.
  • Human-centred: participants remain accountable for decisions and outputs.
  • Policy-aligned: reinforces existing security, privacy, and procurement controls.

Context

Why This Workshop

AI literacy strengthens scientific and government organisations by accelerating routine drafting, clarifying complex ideas, and creating consistent awareness materials—without exposing sensitive information or automating decisions.

Audience

Who Can Benefit

Scientists
Technical Officers
Administrative Staff
Project Teams

Approach

How AI Is Used in This Workshop

Drafting assistance (templates and wording support)
Visual explanation (diagram ideas and structure)
Prototyping support (code snippets and patterns)
Awareness material creation (poster concepts)
No decision-making, no data processing, and no operational workflows.

Tool Highlights

AI Tools Covered

Codex

Assists with development and prototyping by suggesting code patterns, accelerating iteration, and reducing repetitive tasks while keeping humans in control.

Use in sandbox environments; avoid live systems and sensitive repositories.

Napkin.ai

Generates clear visual explanations and workflow diagrams to communicate processes, decision flows, and service concepts without manual diagramming effort.

Keep diagrams generic—no internal network details or classified workflows.

Image Generation AI

Produces poster-ready awareness materials and illustrative concepts to support engagement, with human review ensuring clarity and appropriateness.

Label AI-assisted images clearly and verify they align with branding guidance.

Outcomes

Key Takeaways

  • Recognise where AI drafting and visuals can save time without replacing oversight.
  • Practice shaping prompts that avoid sensitive information and emphasise context.
  • Evaluate outputs critically and document AI-assisted work for transparency.
  • Understand the approval pathways and safeguards required before wider adoption.

Guardrails

Responsible & Safe Use of AI

  • No sensitive, personal, or operational data should be entered into AI tools.
  • All outputs require human review for accuracy, relevance, and appropriateness.
  • Follow organisational security policies and approval pathways before adoption.
  • Use only pre-approved tools and keep records of prompts and outputs where required.
  • Maintain transparency: clearly label AI-assisted materials and cite sources when applicable.
  • Default to minimal data sharing; redact specifics and rely on scenario-based prompts.
  • Seek security and legal guidance before integrating AI into workflows or procurement.
